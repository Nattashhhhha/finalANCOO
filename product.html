GearBox

A gearbox, also known as a transmission, is a mechanical device used to control the speed and torque of a power source, such as an engine or motor. It consists of a series of gears with different sizes and configurations that can be engaged or disengaged to change the speed and direction of rotation.

Worm GearBox

Worm gearboxes are known for their excellent torque transmission capabilities. The design of the worm and worm wheel allows for a large contact area, resulting in efficient power transfer.This makes worm gearboxes suitable for applications that require high torque,such as heavy machinery and industrial equipment. Worm gearboxes can be used in a wide range of applications across various industries. They are commonly employed in industries such as automotive, manufacturing, packaging, material handling, food processing, and more. Their versatility allows for adaptation to different torque and speed requirements.

Helical GearBox

A helical gearbox, also known as a helical gear reducer, is a type of gearbox that uses helical gears to transmit power and torque between rotating shafts. Helical gearboxes can accommodate a wide range of applications due to their versatility. They can transmit power at various angles and can be used in both parallel and right-angle configurations. This flexibility makes them suitable for a diverse range of industries, including automotive, manufacturing, mining, and power generation. It is generally more compact compared to other type of gearboxes with similar power transmission capabilities. This compactness allows for easier integration into machinery and reduces the overall space requirements, making them preferable in applications where space is limited.
